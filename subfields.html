Astrophysics 🪐

Astrophysics is an extremely accessible field, espeically for beginners who should (ideally) posses skills in data science (Python is the primary language of choice for most analysts) and general programming. A vast quanity of open-source data exists through governmental agencies such as NASA and ESA.

Astrophysics Homepage

Quantum Information 💻

Quantum Information Science (QIS) explores how quantum systems store, process, and transmit information. Once the basic theory of quantum computation is understood, much work can be done through the construction and testing of novel quantum algorithms.

Quantum Information Homepage

Condensed Matter and AMO Physics ⚛️

Condensed Matter and AMO physics study the behavior of matter and light on atomic and molecular scales. While such fields tend to rely heavily on in-person experimental work, many simulations, online data sets, and interactives are available for deeper exploration.

Condensed Matter/AMO Homepage

Particle and Nuclear Physics 💥

Particle and Nuclear Physics explore the fundamental constituents of matter and their interactions. Many large experimental collaborations publish their open-source data, analysis pipelines, packages, and simulations, including live event displays! Much of particle physics analysis happens in Python, C++, or ROOT.

Particle and Nuclear Homepage

Mathematical & Engineering Physics 📊

Mathematical physics uses the tools of mathematics to solve problems in theoretical physics. Engineering physics utilizes physics to solve engineering problems, and vice versa. Both of these objectives are often accomplished through complex, high-end simulations, though a variety of tangible resources exist.

Mathematical Physics Homepage

Biophysics and Geophysics 🧬

Biophysics and Geophysics apply physics to understand biological systems and the Earth (respectively). Python packages, simulations, and open datasets (often live feeds from geological or biological systems) can be explored in the context of advanced physics research.

Bio/Geophysics Homepage

Physics Education 📖

Physics Education is the study of how physics is taught and how students learn physics. Physics Education Research (PER) is extremely diverse, and focuses on every aspect of teaching physics, from equity in physics education to designing validated tests of student understanding. This page provides resources/curricula for both educators teaching physics at all levels and those conducting physics education research.

Physics Education Homepage

Experimental Techniques & Statistics 🔍

Experimental Techniques and Statistics covers essential tools used across experimental physics, including data acquisition, error analysis, signal processing, and statistical inference. Many tools are built to supplement the experimental process, improve data analysis techniques, or interface with hardware in the lab.

Experimental Homepage
